My Background

My passion for signal processing began research in senior year of high school in the Kamaleswaran lab at Emory University (now Duke), where I investigated ML models for early classificaiton of Interstitial Lung Disease. I then studied Biomedical Engineering in my freshman year at GT, where I joined the Medical Robotics Club, and began a project on Electrooculagraphy based eye-tracking and blink detection. This project especially made me realize that Electrical Engineering would be a better fit for me, so I changed my major to Electrical Engineering starting Spring of 2026. By this point, I had realized the true scope of the major and was more willing to participate in clubs and projects outside of bioengineering, so I joined the SiliconJackets AMS team, which is focused on transistor-level ASIC design. I'm looking forward to finishing the projects I've started and trying out projects in different industries as well such as autonomous robots & vehicles, aviation, and space.
