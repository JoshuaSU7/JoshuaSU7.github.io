Career Goals

Where I'm headed and why it matters to me.

Vision

The Big Picture

My goal is to my foundation in electrical engineering to design hardware and systems that directly improve people's quality of life. The projects I'm drawn to, from crash detection wearables to human-computer interfaces for paralyzed individuals, all share a common thread: using engineering to improve safety, accessibility, or precision.

I'm particularly interested in careers that bridge low-level hardware design (analog circuits, embedded systems, signal processing) with high-impact applications in medical devices, assistive technology, and safety engineering.

Focus Areas

What I'm Working Toward

Biomedical Devices

Designing wearable sensors and hardware for real-time health monitoring and assistive applications.

Analog & Mixed-Signal IC Design

Developing the low-level circuit building blocks that power precision electronics in medical and safety systems.

Assistive Technology

Building human-computer interfaces and control systems that restore independence to individuals with physical disabilities.

Safety Systems

Applying embedded sensing and signal processing to create proactive safety devices, from crash detection to fall prevention.

Roadmap

My Path Forward

Near-term — Undergrad

Build a Strong Technical Foundation

Complete my ECE degree at Georgia Tech with a focus on signal processing, sensing, and embedded systems. To do this I will complete my threads in signal processing and sensing and also take some computer architecture courses such as ECE3058, and some automation courses such as ECE4550 and ECE4560. Additionally, I will complete my current projects in clubs such as Medical Robotics and SiliconJackets, and obtain a leadership role in both clubs, starting my own project and leading a team. I will also pursue internships in hardware or firmware to get hands-on industry experience.

Mid-term — Early Career

Work on Impactful Hardware Products

Join a company developing wearable medical devices, assistive technology, or safety electronics. Contribute to the full design cycle from circuit design and prototyping through to testing and production. Collaborate with various other engineers to gain exposure to other fields of engineering, and try out many different industries.

Long-term

Lead and Innovate

Take on a senior engineering or research role with the goal of driving product direction. Potentially pursue a graduate degree if it opens doors to deeper research in embedded systems or signal processing.
